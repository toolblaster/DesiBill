Discount Formulas Explained

Double Discount (50% + 20%)

Many people think 50% + 20% = 70%. This is wrong.

1. Start: ₹100
2. Apply 50%: ₹100 - ₹50 = ₹50
3. Apply 20% on ₹50: ₹50 - ₹10 = ₹40
Effective Discount: 60%

Buy X Get Y Free

A classic "Buy 2 Get 1 Free" offer sounds great, but what is the actual %?

Formula: Free / (Buy + Free)
Calc: 1 / (2 + 1) = 1 / 3
Effective Discount: 33.33%

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a double discount?

A double discount occurs when an extra percentage (e.g., "Extra 10% Off") is applied after the initial discount. It is calculated sequentially, not by simply adding the percentages together.

2. How is 'Buy 1 Get 1' calculated?

"Buy 1 Get 1 Free" implies you get 2 items but pay for 1. The math is 1/2 = 0.50, which means you are getting a 50% discount on the total value.

3. What is the formula to find discount percentage?

Use this formula: ((Original Price - Sale Price) / Original Price) * 100. For example, if an item drops from ₹100 to ₹80, the discount is 20%.

4. Does this tool work for GST inclusive prices?

Yes. If your MRP includes GST, the discounted price calculated will also include GST. You can then use our GST Calculator to separate the tax component from the discounted price.
