How to Calculate GST: Formula & Examples

Understanding how to calculate GST manually is useful, even if you use our GST calculator. The GST calculation formula changes based on whether the tax is included or excluded.

1. GST Exclusive (Adding Tax)

Used when tax is added *on top* of the base price.

GST Amount = (Base Price × GST%) / 100
Net Price = Base Price + GST Amount

GST calculation example: For a ₹1,000 item at 18% GST:
₹1,000 × 18% = ₹180 Tax. Total = ₹1,180.

2. GST Inclusive (Reverse Calculation)

Used to find the base price from the MRP.

GST Amount = Total - [Total × {100 / (100 + GST%)}]
Base Price = Total Price - GST Amount

GST calculation example: For a ₹1,180 MRP at 18% GST:
Base Price = ₹1,000. Tax = ₹180.

GST Inclusive vs Exclusive Price Meaning

When creating bills, understanding the difference between GST inclusive vs exclusive pricing is critical to avoid losses.

GST Exclusive Meaning

The price of the goods does not include tax. Tax is calculated on top of this value. This is standard for B2B invoices.

GST Inclusive Meaning

The final price paid by the customer includes the tax component. Shopkeepers selling at MRP use this to back-calculate their net revenue.

Essential GST Invoicing Rules & Compliance Guide

Staying compliant is just as important as calculating the right tax amount. Here is an evergreen guide to ensuring your GST billing is 100% accurate and legal.

Mandatory Invoice Elements

  • GSTIN: Both supplier and buyer (if registered).
  • Invoice Number & Date: Must be consecutive and unique for the financial year.
  • HSN/SAC Code: Mandatory based on business turnover.
  • Tax Breakdown: Clear separation of CGST, SGST, and IGST amounts.

Input Tax Credit (ITC) Basics

ITC is the backbone of the GST system. It allows a registered business to deduct the tax they have already paid on purchases from the tax they need to pay on their sales.

Crucial Rule: To claim ITC, you must have a valid tax invoice from your supplier, and the supplier must have uploaded the invoice in their GSTR-1 return.

Top GST Billing Mistakes to Avoid

Wrong Tax Type: Charging IGST instead of CGST/SGST for intra-state sales (within the same state).
Incorrect HSN: Using the wrong HSN code can lead to incorrect tax rates being applied, resulting in penalties.
Not Reconciling Data: Failing to match your purchase register with GSTR-2B before claiming ITC.
Exclusive vs Inclusive Mix-up: Calculating GST on an MRP that already includes tax (use our "Inclusive" mode to fix this!).

Frequently Asked Questions

1. When to charge IGST?

Charge IGST (Integrated Tax) when the supply of goods or services happens between two different states (Inter-state supply). The rate is equal to the sum of CGST and SGST.

2. Which GST rate applies to my business?

3%: Gold, Silver, Precious metals.
5%: Essential food items, clothing under ₹1000.
12%: Processed foods, mobiles.
18%: Most services, electronics, capital goods.

3. Do I need to split the tax in the calculator?

This GST calculator online gives the Total Tax. If your sale is intra-state (same state), simply divide this tax amount by 2 to get your CGST and SGST values for filing.

4. What is an HSN Code?

HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) is a 6-digit code used to classify goods for taxation. It is mandatory for invoices if annual turnover exceeds ₹5 Crores.
