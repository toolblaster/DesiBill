Margin vs Markup: What's the Difference?

Markup (Profit on Cost)

This is what most shopkeepers use. It is the percentage added to the cost price to get the selling price.

Cost: ₹100
Markup: 20%
Selling Price: ₹120

Gross Margin (Profit on Sale)

This is what accountants use. It shows how much of your sales revenue is actual profit.

Cost: ₹100
Margin: 20%
Selling Price: ₹125

Avoid the "Profit Trap"

Common Mistake: If you buy an item for ₹100 and add a 20% Markup, your price is ₹120.
If you then give a 20% Discount to a customer, you sell it for ₹96.
Result: You lost ₹4!

Always calculate discounts based on Margin (Selling Price), not Cost. Use our tool to find the correct selling price to afford discounts.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the difference between Margin and Markup?

Markup is the percentage profit based on your COST Price. Margin is the percentage profit based on your SELLING Price. Markup is always higher than Margin.

2. How to calculate Selling Price from Cost and Margin?

Formula: Selling Price = Cost Price / (1 - (Margin% / 100)). For example, if Cost is 100 and you want 20% Margin, Selling Price = 100 / 0.8 = 125.

3. Why is Gross Margin important for my business?

Gross Margin tells you the actual percentage of every Rupee earned that you keep as profit. Unlike Markup, it helps you understand if your business is sustainable after paying expenses.

4. If I give a discount, does my margin change?

Yes, drastically. Discounts are reduced from the Selling Price, so they lower your Margin significantly. A 10% discount might wipe out 50% of your net profit depending on your initial markup.
